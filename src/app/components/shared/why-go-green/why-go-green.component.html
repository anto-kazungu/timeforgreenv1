Climate Crisis

Our Planet is Warming

Global temperatures have risen by 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, leading to more frequent extreme weather events, melting ice caps, and rising sea levels. Scientists warn that we have less than a decade to take decisive action.

1.5°C Critical threshold
2030 Deadline for action
Biodiversity Loss

Sixth Mass Extinction

Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction, disrupting ecosystems that have taken millions of years to develop. Every species plays a crucial role in maintaining the delicate balance of our planet's ecosystems.

1M+ Species threatened
75% Habitat loss

Water Scarcity

Our Most Vital Resource

By 2025, half of the world's population will live in water-stressed areas. Pollution, over-extraction, and climate change are threatening clean water access. Every drop counts, and conservation efforts today determine water availability for future generations.

2.2B Lack safe water
50% In water stress by 2025
Plastic Pollution

Choking Our Oceans

Every year, 8 million tons of plastic waste enter our oceans, harming marine life and entering our food chain. Microplastics have been found everywhere from the deepest ocean trenches to the highest mountain peaks.

8M Tons yearly
2050 More plastic than fish

Deforestation

Losing Earth's Lungs

Every minute, an area of forest the size of 27 football fields is destroyed. Deforestation contributes to climate change, destroys habitats, and threatens indigenous communities who depend on forests for their survival.

10M Hectares lost/year
27 Football fields/minute
Air Pollution

The Air We Breathe

Air pollution kills 7 million people every year and affects billions more. From vehicle emissions to industrial pollutants, the air we breathe is increasingly toxic. Clean air is a fundamental human right that we must fight to protect.

7M Deaths annually
91% Breathe polluted air
