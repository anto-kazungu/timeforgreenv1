Climate Crisis

Our planet is warming at an unprecedented rate. Global temperatures have risen by 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, leading to more frequent extreme weather events, melting ice caps, and rising sea levels. Scientists warn that we have less than a decade to take decisive action to prevent catastrophic climate change.

1.5°C Critical warming threshold

Biodiversity Loss

We're experiencing the sixth mass extinction event in Earth's history, but this time it's human-caused. Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction, disrupting ecosystems that have taken millions of years to develop. Every species plays a crucial role in maintaining the delicate balance of our planet's ecosystems.

1M+ Species at risk
water_drop

Water Scarcity

Clean water is becoming increasingly scarce. By 2025, half of the world's population will live in water-stressed areas. Pollution, over-extraction, and climate change are threatening our most vital resource. Every drop counts, and conservation efforts today will determine water availability for future generations.

2.2B People lack safe drinking water

Plastic Pollution

Every year, 8 million tons of plastic waste enter our oceans, harming marine life and entering our food chain. Microplastics have been found in the deepest ocean trenches and highest mountain peaks. If current trends continue, there will be more plastic than fish in the ocean by 2050.

8M Tons of plastic in oceans yearly
park

Deforestation

Forests are the lungs of our planet, yet we're losing them at an alarming rate. Every minute, an area of forest the size of 27 football fields is destroyed. Deforestation contributes to climate change, destroys habitats, and threatens indigenous communities who depend on forests for their survival.

10M Hectares lost annually
factory

Air Pollution

Air pollution kills 7 million people every year and affects billions more. From vehicle emissions to industrial pollutants, the air we breathe is increasingly toxic. Children, elderly, and those with respiratory conditions are particularly vulnerable. Clean air is a fundamental human right that we must fight to protect.

7M Deaths annually from air pollution
